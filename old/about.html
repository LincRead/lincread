Long story

I'm a Norwegian Game Developer who's been obsesses with computer games since I was 6 years old. When I was 13 my neighbor who was 12 and I discussed how to afford more video games to play. What if we make a web site where we review games, maybe companies will send us free promo games? That's what happened. Our post boxes filled up with new games from Sony, Microsoft, EA, Ubisoft etc. We had to find additional writers to get through them all.

After a couple of years writing for a few gaming and eSport sites, and graduating from advance classes, I decided to see if game development could be something for me, so I started my higher education in Experience Production and Interactive Medias at Hamar College University. I initially wanted to be a game designer and make art, but quickly found myself learning to program so I could actually make something to play.

I contemplated joining a start up with fellow students after graduating, but I didn't feel like I had the theory tool box to call myself a decent programmer, so I took two additional years of Computer Science at University of Oslo. During those years I eventually joined a start-up. From there on my life was filled with start-ups, game jams, freelance work, taking lecturing jobs, for a couple of years, trying to gain as much experience as possible

Then I grew out of that phase, fell in love with my Australian partner (we now have a daughter together), and moved to Australia. These days I'm still taking remote freelance work for Norwegian companies. I'm also doing all kinds of non-geeky stuff like playing soccer, climbing a hill with the pram, and exploring how it is to live like an aussie.

At the moment I'm looking for new career opportunities, so please let me know if you have a suggestion.
